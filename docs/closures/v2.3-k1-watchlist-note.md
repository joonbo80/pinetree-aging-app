K1 Party Alias Normalization is on WATCHLIST.

Full K1 implementation is deferred. The conditional check ran against

baseline statement + transaction data. The combined evidence does not
yet meet the recurring drift threshold for full normalization, but
several candidates are significant enough that they must not be
forgotten.

## 2. Purpose of K1

K1 normalizes the same business entity that appears under slightly
different `partyKey` spellings across ERP transactions and statement
sources.

Without normalization, the same business can split across two or more
`partyKey` values, and the Statement Collection Workbench treats them
as different parties. That hides real open balances and weakens
collection follow-up.

Why it matters: small volume now, but the corporate suffix pattern

## 5. Candidates Considered But Excluded

The following looked like alias drift on first scan but were
re-classified as ERP naming variants rather than statement drift.
They are intentionally left out of the K1 watchlist:

- `hapag-lloyd` and `hapag-lloyd-canada`
- `king-bros` and `king-bros-limited`

These cases come from ERP-side spelling differences. There is no
evidence yet that the same business is split across statement and
transaction sources for these parties.

## 6. Decision

| Question | Decision |
|---|---|
| K1 full alias normalization right now? | NO |
| K1 candidates worth tracking? | YES (3 above) |
| K1 status | WATCHLIST |
| Trigger to revisit | May statement files arrive |
| Re-evaluation criteria | recurring statement-side drift confirmed |

Rationale:

- Recurring drift count is currently below the threshold for full
  implementation.
- LEMOND is high impact, but a single high-impact case does not
  justify a broad fuzzy normalization system.
- Broad fuzzy normalization carries silent-merge risk for accounting
  workflows. AR/AP separation and currency separation rules must hold
  after any K1 design.
- A safer next step is a small manual alias map (K1-Lite), but only
  after the next round of statement evidence confirms that statement
  drift is recurring, not one-off.

## 7. Recommended Next Direction

Two-step recommendation:

1. Wait for the next statement batch (specifically the May statement
   files, or a fresh export equivalent).
2. If statement-side drift recurs across multiple parties, draft
   K1-Lite as a focused micro-spec:
   - Manual alias map only.
   - No fuzzy auto-merge.
   - Selector-level normalization to preserve raw snapshot data.
   - Invariants on map content and AR/AP and currency safety.

If statement drift does not recur, K1 stays deferred and the project
moves to v2.4 planning.

## 8. Out-of-Scope For K1-Lite (if it is built later)

When K1-Lite is eventually drafted, it will explicitly NOT include:

- Fuzzy string matching (Levenshtein, etc.).
- Auto-merge by name similarity.
- ML-based or heuristic clustering.
- Cross-currency alias merge.
- Server-side or SharePoint-side alias management (that is Phase 3).

## 9. Standing Rules Reaffirmed By K1 Watchlist

- Accounting safety overrides convenience: silent merge of distinct
  party keys is not acceptable without explicit mapping.
- AR/AP separation rule continues to apply after any future
  normalization.
- USD/CAD separation rule continues to apply after any future
  normalization.
- Re-evaluation requires data evidence, not assumption.
